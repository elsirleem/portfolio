## Cover Letter Style & Tone

### Structure (all letters follow this pattern)
1. **Opening hook** — A bold, thematic statement about the domain or role's appeal. Never "I am applying for...". Best hooks are philosophical (data, technology, impact).
2. **Personal positioning** — Current status (Erasmus Mundus MSc, Green Deal), international background (NL/Finland/Italy), core technical strengths.
3. **Relevant experience** — 1–2 paragraphs tying specific past work to the role. Always anchor on MetricMind (Schuberg Philis). Add File Solutions for financial/data integrity angles. Add SwanIT for sustainability or data transformation angles.
4. **Why this company** — Specific, genuine reasons: tech stack, domain impact, learning structure, sustainability alignment. Never generic.
5. **Closing** — Confident but humble. Express excitement, mention what Salim brings.

**Tone:** Professional but personal, confident, technically grounded, internationally aware, sustainability-conscious.
**Length:** 4–5 paragraphs, one page max.

### Opening Hook Examples (by role type)
- **Data Engineering (general):** "Data becomes valuable only when it is reliable, usable, and trusted enough to support real decisions." (ING DE)
- **Data Engineering (financial):** "Raw data only becomes valuable when someone can trust it, understand it, and use it to make informed decisions." (Rabobank — strongest hook)
- **Graduate/Trainee programs:** Lead with what's unique about the program structure — rotational model, sustainability, cross-functional exposure.
- **Supply Chain / Ops roles:** Lead with what connects tech, data, and operational improvement to meaningful outcomes.
- **Avoid:** "I am excited to apply..." as an opener (used in ABN AMRO — weaker).

### Key Recurring Themes Across All Letters

- **MetricMind** — The anchor story. Covers: Python, FastAPI, PostgreSQL, data pipelines, KPI modelling, GitHub/GitLab/Jira ingestion, decision support, thesis research.
